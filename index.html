Research

My research explores how language models can interpret sensor data while remaining secure, robust, and privacy-preserving. I focus on multimodal human activity recognition, adversarial attacks, data poisoning detection, and interpretable AI for healthcare and consumer devices.

01Sensor–Text Alignment

Contrastive learning between wearable sensor embeddings and natural-language activity descriptions.

02AI Security

Data poisoning, adversarial perturbations, embedding attacks, and robust defenses for HAR systems.

03Health AI

Federated and interpretable SLMs for personalized health support on consumer devices.

04Smart Environments

Fall detection, emergency response, and LLM-integrated healthcare cyber-physical systems.

Breaking Sensor-Text Alignment: Cross-Modal Attack on Contrastive Multimodal Human Activity Recognition

IEEE/CVF Conference on Computer Vision and Pattern Recognition, 2026

Introduces a cross-modal embedding poisoning attack that disrupts contrastive alignment between wearable sensor data and text descriptions across WISDM, MotionSense, HHAR, and WESAD.

project page soon

This work studies a search-based text-embedding perturbation attack during inference. The attack reduces correct sensor-text cosine similarity, increases contrastive loss, and causes semantic drift in shared embedding spaces.
