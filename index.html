Malithi Mithsara

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Southern Illinois University Carbondale. My research focuses on human activity recognition, wearable AI, multimodal sensor-language learning, large and small language models, and trustworthy AI for healthcare and smart environments.

I am especially interested in secure and privacy-preserving sensing systems, including adversarial attacks, data poisoning detection, contrastive sensor-text alignment, and federated small language models for personalized health support.

Breaking Sensor-Text Alignment: Cross-Modal Attack on Contrastive Multimodal Human Activity Recognition

CVPR 2026

This work studies a cross-modal inference-time attack that perturbs text-side embeddings in contrastive multimodal HAR systems, reducing correct sensor-text cosine similarity and causing semantic drift.

Project Website

Adaptive and Robust Data Poisoning Detection and Sanitization in Wearable IoT Systems using Large Language Models

IEEE Transactions on Human-Machine Systems, under review, 2026

This project explores LLM-assisted detection and sanitization of poisoned activity labels in wearable IoT systems.

Paper

A Comprehensive Survey of Large Language Models for Human Activity Recognition: Opportunities, Challenges, and Future Directions

IEEE Transactions on Computational Intelligence, under review, 2026

A survey of how LLMs can support sensor-based HAR through zero/few-shot learning, contextual reasoning, sensor knowledge learning, and deployment strategies.

Preprint

Federated Small Language Models for Personalized and Interpretable Women's Health Support on Consumer Devices

IEEE Transactions on Consumer Electronics, under review, 2026

This project uses feature-to-text conversion and federated LoRA fine-tuning of SLMs for personalized menstrual phase prediction and concise health feedback.

About

I am a PhD student in Computer Science at Southern Illinois University, Carbondale. My research focuses on human activity recognition, multimodal sensor-language learning, trustworthy AI, and privacy-preserving language-model systems for healthcare and smart environments.

My current work explores how large and small language models can interpret wearable sensor data, detect adversarial or poisoned labels, and support robust multimodal reasoning in real-world sensing applications.
